Problem

Most cold outreach fails before it's read. Generic openers, feature dumps, and templated subject lines signal immediately that nobody did their homework. Reps know this — so they either spend 20 minutes researching each contact before writing, or they send something generic and hope for the best. Neither scales. The research takes too long to do manually for every contact on a list, and the generic approach wastes the list entirely.

Solution
1
Signal-first research. Before writing a single word, the skill pulls everything available on the contact: lead list score rationale, ZoomInfo enrichment, LinkedIn profile details, and recent company news. It looks for something specific — an accomplishment they highlighted, a tool they mentioned, an initiative they're running — and builds the message around that.
2
Prioritized hook selection. Not all signals are equal. The skill ranks available hooks and leads with the strongest one automatically. An AI or ML mandate in someone's title beats a recent role change, which beats a company announcement, which beats a generic industry angle. The message always opens with the most specific, relevant hook available.
3
Consistent voice, every time. The skill is trained on real outreach examples that have generated replies — capturing a specific style: warm but direct, curious not pushy, short enough to read without scrolling on a phone. Every draft passes a voice check before output. No em dashes, no "I'd love to connect," no feature lists in a first message.
4
Account-aware context. When MongoDB is already deployed at an account, the skill knows. Instead of pitching cold, messages reference that other teams are already running on Atlas and offer to share what they are seeing. This turns a cold outreach into something closer to a peer referral.
5
Flexible output. For 1 to 5 contacts, drafts appear inline in chat for immediate copy-paste. For larger batches, the skill appends email and LinkedIn draft columns directly to the existing lead list spreadsheet so everything stays in one place.
Hook priority order
1
AI or ML keyword in title
They are actively building or owning AI and data work. Lead with genuine curiosity about what they are building. Never assume they know the product.
2
New to role in last 6 months
New leaders evaluate vendors early and move fast. Acknowledge the new chapter naturally, without making it feel like a trigger you found in a database.
3
Company-level announcement
Recent news, product launch, AI initiative, or funding round. Connect it to a relevant capability rather than just mentioning you saw the news.
4
Generic signal
Default to Atlas Database angle or an open "what are you building" question. Never fabricate specificity that does not exist.

Impact
⏱
Research time eliminated
20 minutes of manual LinkedIn research per contact replaced by automated signal gathering before the first word is written.
🎯
Every message is specific
The hook is always grounded in something real about the contact. No generic openers, no mail-merge feel, no feature dumps in a first message.
🔁
Consistent voice at scale
Whether drafting 1 message or 20, the tone, length, and style are identical. The rep's voice is codified into the skill, not approximated each time.

Feeds directly into the lead list

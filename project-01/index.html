Problem

Sales reps are notoriously bad at Salesforce hygiene, and it is not really their fault. After a high-stakes customer meeting, the last thing anyone wants to do is spend 30 to 45 minutes filling out opportunity fields from memory. The result is incomplete records, outdated discovery notes, and leadership flying blind on deal status. Yet accurate opportunity documentation is critical for forecasting, deal reviews, SE handoffs, and keeping the entire revenue team aligned on what is actually happening in a deal.

Solution
1
Meeting capture via Granola. Every customer call is automatically transcribed and summarized by Granola, including AI-generated notes, action items, and key discussion points. No manual note-taking required.
2
Skill built in Claude. A custom Claude skill was written to know exactly which Granola meetings to pull, how to map transcript content to Salesforce opportunity fields, and how to apply consistent rules: account naming, sales motion classification, value driver taxonomy, and the 3 Why's framework.
3
Structured output generated automatically. Claude reads all relevant meeting notes for an opportunity, extracts and synthesizes every required field including opportunity name, sales motion, product type, discovery capture sheet, and 3 Why's, then outputs a clean formatted Word document ready to share or paste into Salesforce.
4
Human review, not human labor. The rep's job shifts from writing to reviewing. Unknown fields are flagged explicitly and nothing is fabricated. The rep spends minutes reviewing instead of 30 or more minutes writing from scratch.

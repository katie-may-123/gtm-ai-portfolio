Problem

Building a quality prospect list manually is one of the most time-consuming tasks in a sales rep's week. Reading through LinkedIn profiles, identifying the right contacts, enriching them with emails and phone numbers, researching company signals to make outreach relevant, and then uploading everything into a sequencing tool can take hours per account. And even after all that work, the list is often incomplete, inconsistently scored, and missing the context that would make outreach actually land.

Solution
1
Structured intake. The skill opens with a short set of questions: account name, number of targets, job function, seniority level, any additional filters, and whether the rep wants an Excel file (to work from) or a CSV (to upload to a sequence tool like Outreach or Salesloft). All in one message, no back-and-forth.
2
ZoomInfo contact sourcing. The skill queries ZoomInfo for contacts matching the ICP — pulling name, title, seniority, email, phone, LinkedIn URL, and department. Invalid roles are filtered out before scoring: Site Reliability Engineering, Frontend, UX, and Mobile titles are automatically excluded as non-buyers for the database infrastructure use case.
3
Web search enrichment. Two rounds of research run in parallel — company-level signals (AI initiatives, hiring, funding, strategic announcements) applied to all contacts, and contact-level signals (new to role, published content, public AI/data commentary) applied individually to the highest-priority targets first.
4
Custom scoring logic. Each contact receives a 1-10 score using a defined, transparent model that weighs seniority, recency in role, AI/ML in title, and both contact and company signals. The scoring flips into ICP override mode when the rep specifies a narrow seniority band, so signals become the primary differentiator within the tier.
5
Formatted output. Contacts are sorted by score and delivered either as a color-coded Excel workbook (one tab per account, summary sheet, dropdown status tracking) or a flat CSV formatted for direct SalesLoft import.

Impact
⏱
Hours saved per account
What used to take 2 to 4 hours of manual LinkedIn research, enrichment, and list building now takes under a minute.
🎯
Built-in prioritization
Every contact is scored before the rep sees it. No more guessing who to call first — Tier A contacts float to the top automatically.
📋
Sequence-ready output

How we could continue building on this
Next up
Automated outreach drafting per contact
Take the score rationale column and automatically generate a personalized cold email and LinkedIn message for each Tier A and B contact, grounded in their specific signals.
Next up
Direct SalesLoft push via API
Skip the CSV download entirely. Once a list is generated and reviewed, push contacts directly into a SalesLoft cadence with one confirmation step.
Idea
ICP customization per company
Right now the N/A filter and scoring weights are tuned for a database infrastructure use case. Building company-specific ICP profiles would let any seller use this without manual configuration.
Idea
More granular scoring signals
Incorporate intent data (6sense, Bombora), recent job postings at the account, tech stack signals from ZoomInfo, and LinkedIn activity recency to push score differentiation beyond what web search can detect.
Idea
Automated re-scoring on role changes
Run a weekly job that detects when contacts on an existing list have changed roles, then resurface them as new Tier A targets with a "new to role" flag.

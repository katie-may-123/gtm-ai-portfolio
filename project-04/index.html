Pre-meeting prep brief

A one-page briefing document generated before every customer call, pulling from meeting history, opportunity context, lead signals, and recent account news.

Built withGranola API · Claude skill · Web search
OutputWord document (.docx)
First runApril 14, 2026
Problem

Walking into a customer meeting unprepared is one of the easiest ways to lose credibility. But proper prep takes real time: scanning back through old meeting notes, remembering what you committed to last time, pulling up recent company news, and figuring out what questions to ask next. Most reps skip it when calendars are packed, or they do a surface-level scan that misses the details that actually matter. The result is meetings that feel reactive rather than consultative, and discovery gaps that stay open for weeks longer than they should.

Solution
1
Flexible triggering. The skill responds to natural language. "Prep me for my meetings today", "I have a meeting with [name] at [account], help me prep", or "What do I need to know before my [account] call". It identifies the right meeting from Granola, and if Granola has no calendar data, it falls back to meeting history and asks for any details it needs rather than failing silently.
2
Multi-source synthesis. The skill pulls from every relevant data source in the stack: full Granola meeting history for the account, the opportunity doc (if one exists and is relevant to this meeting), lead list signals for the contact, and a targeted web search for recent account news. Sources are only included if they add something real. Empty sections are omitted rather than padded.
3
Opportunity-aware discovery questions. If an opportunity doc exists for the account, the skill surfaces which Discovery Capture Sheet fields are still marked Unknown and maps each one to a specific question to ask in the meeting. It turns gaps in the CRM into a concrete agenda item rather than leaving them invisible.
4
Editable Word doc output. The brief is output as a .docx file so the rep can take notes directly in the document, annotate it before the call, or share it with an SE or manager. A rep could print it and walk in with it, or open it on a second monitor during the meeting.
5
Multi-meeting support. When "prep me for today" returns more than one customer call, the skill asks whether to generate one combined document or a separate brief per meeting, then delivers whichever format the rep requests.
Data sources
📝
Granola meeting history
All prior notes for the account, synthesized across the full relationship, not just the last meeting.
📄
Opportunity doc
Sales motion, use case, current/future state, 3 Why's status, and Unknown discovery fields (if a relevant opp doc exists).
🎯
Lead list signals
Contact tier, score rationale, and signals (new to role, AI title, data mandate) if the contact appeared in a prior lead list.
🔍
Web search
2-3 bullets of recent account news from the last 30 days only. Omitted entirely if nothing material is found.
Document structure
PrepBrief_[Account]_[YYYY-MM-DD].docx Editable · Note-friendly
Who you're meeting Name, title, time in role, lead list tier and score, key signals
Where we left off Relationship summary, open action items (rep's and theirs), recurring themes across meetings
Opportunity status Sales motion, product type, use case, what we know, what's still unknown, 3 Why's status
Recent account news 2-3 bullets max, last 30 days only. Omitted if nothing material found.
Recommended topics Open-ended suggestions based on deal stage, plus specific discovery questions mapped to Unknown opp doc fields
Notes Blank. For the rep to fill in during or before the call

Impact
⏱
Prep time eliminated
What used to take 20 to 30 minutes of scanning notes, emails, and LinkedIn takes 15 seconds to generate.
🎯
Nothing falls through the cracks
Open action items from prior meetings are surfaced automatically. No more starting a call and realizing you forgot to follow up on something.
🧭
Discovery gaps become an agenda
Unknown fields from the opp doc are converted into specific questions to ask, so every meeting moves the deal forward rather than covering ground already covered.
📝
Built for how reps actually work
A Word doc the rep can annotate, print, or open alongside a Zoom call. Not a dashboard they have to navigate mid-meeting.
How we could continue building on this
Next up
Account health tracker
The prep brief is a point-in-time snapshot. The next step is a living view of the deal that synthesizes all meetings over time and surfaces what's changed, what's stalled, and what the recommended next action is.
Next up
Automated morning push via Zapier
Rather than prompting the skill manually, a Zapier automation fires every morning, generates briefs for the day's customer calls, and emails them before the rep's first meeting. No prompt required.
Idea
Salesforce opportunity integration
Pull stage, close date, ARR, and activity history directly from Salesforce to enrich the opportunity status section, without the rep having to manually maintain an opp doc.
Idea
Email thread context
Surface tone and relationship signals from prior email exchanges with the contact. Helpful for understanding where the relationship stands beyond what meeting notes capture.
Idea
ICP feedback loop
After each meeting, log the outcome and use it to refine lead scoring over time, so the contacts who most often lead to productive meetings get surfaced higher on future lead lists.
